How do i study in USA?

Studying in the United States offers a unique and enriching academic experience, making it a popular choice for international students. To embark on this educational journey, aspiring students must follow a systematic approach. Begin by researching universities and programs that align with your academic and career goals. The U.S. boasts a diverse range of institutions renowned for their academic excellence, cutting-edge research, and vibrant campus life. Once you've identified potential universities, focus on meeting their admission requirements, which typically include standardized test scores, academic transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a compelling personal statement. It's crucial to start the application process well in advance, paying close attention to deadlines. Additionally, explore available scholarships and financial aid options to ease the financial burden. Engaging with the local culture, preparing for standardized tests like the TOEFL or GRE, and understanding the visa application process are essential steps. By meticulously navigating these steps, you can unlock the doors to a world-class education in the United States, fostering personal and professional growth.

Student Visa Requirements For The US

Getting your visa is the next step after choosing where in the US to study, which is a crucial decision. There are three primary categories of US Student visas to select from, and each has its own specifications, purpose and requirements:

F-1 Visa: With an F-1 student visa, you are able to pay for your education at US universities or colleges with help of funding from friends, family, or your own finances. When your academic year is in progress, you are also allowed to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week on campus.

M-1 Visa: You can apply for an M-1 visa to study at institutions recognised by the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) if you intend to pursue vocational education in the USA. With an M-1 student visa, you can only work on campus for a maximum of six months and are subject to the same employment restrictions as F-1 and J-1 visa holders.

J-1 Visa: Using this student visa, you are able to take part in work- and attend study-related exchange programmes at US colleges and universities that have been authorised by the Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs of the US Department of State. Your programme must be sponsored by a nonprofit or educational institution. You can only work on campus while on a J-1 visa, which has the same work restrictions as an F-1 visa.

You will require an I-20 certificate (or IAP66 in some cases) form to apply for an F-1 or M-1 visa and a DS-2019 form to apply for a J-1 visa. Each form covers personal information, albeit they vary significantly based on the visa such as full name, SEVIS ID numbers, birthdate, academic programme, English proficiency test scores, financial sponsors, etc.

Some universities will give you an I-20 or DS-2019 together with your admissions letter, particularly if you have evidence that you have the resources to cover at least one year of academic study and are exempt from making a deposit. Other US colleges would not release an I-20 or DS-2019 until funding has been verified, any institutional financial awards have been made, and a deposit has been paid.

Education System in USA

Level I – Bachelor’s degree or Undergraduate: In the USA, a Bachelor’s degree course is regarded as an UnderGraduate (UG) course. Usually, the duration of the UG courses in the USA is four years. The first two years of study comprise a variety of classes in different subjects. The most common subjects that are being taught in the first two years of UG courses are history, arts, literature, science and social sciences. May international students in the US choose a community college to complete the first two years of UG prerequisite courses. These students obtain an AA (Associate of Arts) degree and then transfer to a four-year university or college. Apart from the above mandatory courses in the UG, a ‘major’ is the specific field of study in which your degree is focused. In order to meet the degree requirements of the major course, you must take a certain number of courses in the chosen field. The major has to be chosen by the beginning of your third year of the UG course. One of the unique characteristics of the American education system is that you can change the major course multiple times. In simple words, the American education system is very flexible.

Level 2 – Master’s Degree or a Graduate: International students from only a limited number of countries are allowed to pursue Master’s courses in the US. Usually, the duration of the Master’s courses in America is one year or two years. Most of the master’s courses are merely based on classroom teaching, and each student must prepare a long research paper called Master’s thesis or complete a Master’s project. For admission to a gradation program, applicants are required to sit exams such as GRE or GMAT, LSAT for law, and MCAT for medical programs. Students can even Study in USA Without IELTS.

Level 3 – Doctoral Degree: Usually, the duration of PhD in America is three years. However, for international students, a Doctorate degree may take up to five or six years to complete. Students can enter Doctoral degree courses with a Master’s degree. The majority of universities in the US enrol new students during the autumn. Therefore, international students can apply for admission during this season.
